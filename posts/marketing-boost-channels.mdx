## Overview

Marketing Boost Channels coordinate your team to amplify content reach. When you publish a post, your team gets notified to engage, share, and help boost visibility.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Boost Channel">
    Set up a channel and group team members together.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish Content">
    When you publish or schedule a post, it becomes eligible for boosting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Team Gets Notified">
    Members receive alerts via Slack, email, or in-app notifications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Coordinated Engagement">
    Team members like, comment, and share the content.
    
    <Check>
    Engagement metrics show team participation and boost impact.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setting Up a Boost Channel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Boost Channels">
    Go to **Marketing Boost Channels** → **New Channel**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name Your Channel">
    Choose a descriptive name like "Leadership Team" or "Sales Amplification."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Team Members">
    Select which team members should receive boost notifications.
    
    <Tip>
    Create different channels for different content types—executives for company news, sales team for product content.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Notifications">
    Choose how members are notified: Slack, email, or in-app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Guidelines">
    Optionally add engagement guidelines for team members.
    
    <Check>
    Your boost channel appears in the list and is ready to use.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Notification Options

### Slack Integration

Notify your team via Slack:
- Dedicated channel for boost requests
- Direct messages for urgent content
- One-click engagement links

### Email Notifications

Receive boost requests via email:
- Daily digest of posts to engage with
- Immediate notifications for priority content

### In-App Notifications

Get notified within Ordinal:
- Dashboard notifications
- Mobile push notifications

## Tracking Engagement

Monitor your team's boost participation:
- Who engaged with what content
- Engagement timing and quality
- Impact on reach and performance

<Check>
The boost dashboard shows aggregate participation rates and individual team member activity.
</Check>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Engage authentically">
    Don't just like—add meaningful comments that contribute to discussion.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Spread out engagements">
    Stagger team engagements over time rather than all at once for more natural-looking activity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Match expertise to content">
    Have team members engage with content that aligns with their professional expertise.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Team not receiving notifications">
    Check notification preferences for each team member. Ensure Slack integration is properly connected if using Slack notifications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Low participation rates">
    Make boost requests easy to act on. Include direct links and clear calls-to-action in notifications.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
