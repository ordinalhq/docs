---
title: "Deleting Posts"
icon: "trash"
description: "Manage archived posts and restore or permanently delete them"
---

When you delete a post, it's moved to **Trash** rather than being permanently removed. Archived posts stay in Trash for 30 days before being automatically deleted.

## Accessing Trash

From the Calendar view, open **Trash** from the sidebar under **All Posts** to view all archived posts in your workspace. Posts are listed with when they were archived and who archived them.

## Restoring posts

To restore an archived post:

1. Open the post from the Trash page
2. Click **Restore** in the alert banner when viewing an archived post

Restored posts return to your calendar and can be edited and scheduled as normal.

## Permanently deleting

Archived posts are automatically permanently deleted after 30 days. To delete immediately:

1. Open the archived post
2. Click **Delete Immediately** in the alert banner
3. Confirm the action

<Warning>
  Permanent deletion cannot be undone. The post and all its content will be removed from Ordinal.
</Warning>

## What gets archived

- **Posts and ideas** — Both can be archived from the same menu
- **Scheduled posts** — Archiving cancels the scheduled publish
- **Published posts** — By default, archiving in Ordinal does not remove the post from social platforms. To also remove it from LinkedIn, see [Deleting live LinkedIn posts](#deleting-live-linkedin-posts).

## Deleting live LinkedIn posts

When you move a post to Trash or permanently delete it, Ordinal can also delete the live post from LinkedIn in the same step. This is useful when you want to fully remove a published post — not just hide it from your Ordinal calendar.

This option only appears for posts that have already been published to a LinkedIn profile or company page connected to your workspace.

To delete the live LinkedIn post:

1. Open the post and click **Move to Trash** or **Delete Immediately**.
2. In the confirmation modal, under **Delete live post on the following channels as well?**, select the LinkedIn channel.
3. Confirm the action.

Use **View Post** next to each channel to open the live post on LinkedIn before deleting.

<Warning>
  Deleting the live post on LinkedIn cannot be undone. Engagement (likes, comments, reposts) on the live post is permanently lost.
</Warning>

If the LinkedIn channel is not selected, the post is only removed from Ordinal and stays live on LinkedIn.
