## Overview

Schedule your posts to publish at optimal times for maximum engagement. Set it and forget it—Ordinal handles the rest.

## Scheduling a Post

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Your Post">
    Create a new post or open an existing draft from your Posts list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Schedule">
    Click the **Schedule** button in the composer toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Date and Time">
    Choose your preferred date and time from the calendar picker.
    
    <Tip>
    Click "Best Time" to let Ordinal suggest optimal posting times based on your audience's activity.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Timezone">
    Verify the timezone is correct. Ordinal uses your account timezone by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Scheduling">
    Click **Schedule Post** to confirm.
    
    <Check>
    Your post appears in the Calendar with a "Scheduled" status and countdown timer.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Scheduling Options

### Specific Date & Time

Choose exactly when your post goes live:
- Select date from calendar
- Set precise time
- Posts publish within seconds of scheduled time

### Best Time to Post

Let Ordinal suggest optimal times:
- Based on your audience's activity
- Platform-specific recommendations
- Historical engagement data

<Tip>
Best Time recommendations improve over time as Ordinal learns from your posting history and engagement patterns.
</Tip>

### Queue

Add posts to your queue:
- Set recurring time slots
- Posts publish in order
- Automatic spacing between posts

## Managing Scheduled Posts

### View Scheduled Posts

Find all scheduled posts in:
- **Calendar** - Visual timeline
- **Posts** → **Scheduled** - List view
- **Profile view** - Per-profile schedule

### Edit Scheduled Posts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Post">
    Locate the scheduled post in Calendar or Posts list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open and Edit">
    Click to open, make your changes to content or media.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click **Save** to keep the same schedule, or **Reschedule** to change the time.
    
    <Check>
    The post updates with your changes while maintaining its scheduled time.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Cancel Scheduled Posts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Post">
    Find and open the scheduled post.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unschedule or Delete">
    Click **Unschedule** to return it to drafts, or **Delete** to remove it entirely.
    
    <Warning>
    Deleting is permanent. Use Unschedule if you might want to use the content later.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Bulk Scheduling

Schedule multiple posts at once:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Posts">
    In the Posts list, check the boxes next to multiple drafts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Schedule Selected">
    The bulk scheduling dialog opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Method">
    Select your scheduling approach:
    - **Stagger** - Automatic spacing (e.g., 2 hours apart)
    - **Specific times** - Set each post's time manually
    - **Add to queue** - Use your preset time slots
    
    <Check>
    All selected posts appear in your Calendar with their scheduled times.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Post published at wrong time">
    Check your account timezone in Settings → Profile. Ensure it matches your intended timezone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Post failed to publish">
    Check the Posts list for error details. Common causes: expired authorization, platform outage, or content policy violation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't find my scheduled post">
    Use Calendar filters to show all profiles. The post may be scheduled for a different profile than expected.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
