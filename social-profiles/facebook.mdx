## Overview

Connect your Facebook pages to Ordinal to schedule posts, manage engagement, and track performance.

## Connecting Facebook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Add Profile">
    Go to **Social Profiles** â†’ **Add Profile** in your dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Facebook">
    Choose **Facebook** from the list of available platforms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log In to Facebook">
    Enter your Facebook credentials in the popup window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Pages">
    Choose which Facebook pages you want to connect from the list of pages you manage.
    
    <Tip>
    You can connect multiple pages at once. Select all the pages you want to manage through Ordinal.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant Permissions">
    Review and accept the requested permissions. Ordinal needs these to publish and manage content on your behalf.
    
    <Check>
    Your Facebook pages appear in the Social Profiles list with a green "Connected" status.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported Content Types

- **Text posts** - Status updates
- **Images** - Single or multiple photos
- **Videos** - Native video uploads
- **Links** - URL shares with previews
- **Events** - Event announcements
- **Albums** - Photo collections

## Features

### Page Management

Manage multiple Facebook pages from a single dashboard. Switch between pages easily and maintain consistent posting schedules.

### Engagement Tracking

Monitor comments, reactions, and shares directly within Ordinal.

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Optimal posting frequency">
    1-2 posts per day is optimal for most pages to maintain engagement without overwhelming followers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Video content">
    Native video uploads perform better than YouTube links. Upload videos directly for better reach.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Engagement">
    Respond to comments promptly to boost your post's visibility in the algorithm.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Page not appearing in list">
    You must have admin or editor access to a Facebook page to connect it. Check your page roles in Facebook settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Permissions error">
    If you see permission errors, try disconnecting and reconnecting your Facebook account, ensuring you accept all requested permissions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Posts not publishing">
    Verify your page isn't restricted by Facebook. Check your Page Quality tab in Facebook for any issues.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
