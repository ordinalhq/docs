## Overview

Connect your Webflow site to Ordinal to publish blog posts and content directly to your website's CMS.

## Prerequisites

<Note>
Webflow CMS is required. Sites on the free plan without CMS cannot be connected.
</Note>

Your Webflow site needs:
- An active CMS plan
- At least one collection for blog posts
- API access enabled

## Connecting Webflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Add Profile">
    Go to **Social Profiles** â†’ **Add Profile** in your dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Webflow">
    Choose **Webflow** from the list of available platforms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Access">
    Log in to your Webflow account and authorize Ordinal to access your sites.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Site and Collection">
    Choose the Webflow site and CMS collection where you want to publish content.
    
    <Tip>
    Select your blog posts collection if you're primarily using Ordinal for content marketing.
    </Tip>
    
    <Check>
    Your Webflow site appears in the Social Profiles list with a green "Connected" status.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported Features

- **Blog posts** - Publish to your CMS collections
- **Content updates** - Update existing items
- **Draft management** - Save as draft before publishing

## Use Cases

### Synchronized Content

Keep your social media and blog content synchronized:
- Draft a blog post in Ordinal
- Schedule social posts to promote it
- Publish everything at once

### Content Repurposing

Turn your social content into blog posts:
- Expand tweet threads into articles
- Convert LinkedIn posts to blog content

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Site not appearing">
    Ensure your Webflow site has CMS enabled. Free sites without CMS cannot be connected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Collection not syncing">
    Check that your collection has the required fields (title, content, etc.) and that API access is enabled in Webflow site settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Publishing failed">
    Verify your Webflow plan allows API publishing and that you haven't exceeded your CMS item limits.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
